ABCsbook

5.75 × 5.75 in, 62 pages
2022

In the spring of 2022, I took a class in which all the students created letterforms expressing specific words, such as fauxliage, FOMO, and dispronunciation.

Given the project's concern with linguistic definitions, I compiled all our designs into a faux children's book.

Each student's approach to designing their letterforms could broadly be classified into one of three categories: photographic, reproduction-based, or hand-drawn. Consequently, I broke the book down into three smaller books, each color-coded and corresponding to a category, and then spiral bound them all together.

Each book also boasted its own reading direction — left to right, top to bottom, and right to left — requiring the reader to reorient the book for every category.
